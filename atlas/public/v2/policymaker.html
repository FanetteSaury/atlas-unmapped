Why this cohort matters

Programme rationale

Across the country, 88% of working-age youth earn income outside formal credentialing systems. Atlas builds an interoperable skills passport that maps informal experience to ISCO-08 occupation codes and ESCO skill clusters, surfacing each young person's first formal-economy fingerprint.

Cohort signals on this page are intended for programme design, not outreach. Individual identifiers and contact data are not surfaced. To engage candidates, NGO partners can request a Squad introduction through the consent-based intake flow described in the Methodology tab.

AI fluency stratification

Distribution of AI Tier (0–4) across the verified cohort. AI fluency is currently the highest-leverage individual skill in the global labour market and is unmeasured in any other public dataset for LIC + LMIC youth.

AI Tier definitions

Atlas methodology
TierProfileWage premium
0 — UnfamiliarNo AI tool exposure0%
1 — Curious1–2 trial uses, no iteration+5%
2 — ActiveWeekly+ use, iterates, has go-to use cases+20%
3 — Power userMulti-tool, multimodal, verifies output+50%
4 — BuilderBuilds workflows, automations or code on AI+100–300%
Policy implication. Raising the cohort's modal AI Tier from 1 to 2 (a single-tier shift for the median worker) plausibly delivers more aggregate wage uplift than any single-skill training programme tested in the past decade in this country. Atlas measures and tracks this lever; existing labour data does not.

Durable-task strategy

For high-risk occupations (auto-risk > 70%), cohort training should prioritise the durable adjacent tasks — interpersonal service, judgement under context, multilingual customer relationships, and AI orchestration — rather than the exposed routine tasks themselves.

Data refresh policy

Annual cadence. Source data is refreshed once per year, aligned to the publication calendars of the primary providers (World Bank WDI April-May, ILOSTAT rolling, ILO flagship January, BBS / GSS / GSO Q4 country releases, WBL annual edition).

  • Last refresh: 26 April 2026
  • Next scheduled refresh: April 2027
  • Maintainer: Atlas data team — annual provider sweep, not a live API

Atlas-original cohort metrics (Atlas-Card population, AI Tier distribution, ESCO detection frequency) are produced from quest completions and refreshed at the same annual cadence to maintain version-stability for cited research.
